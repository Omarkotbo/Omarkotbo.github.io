Infinities: Mathematicians Measure Infinities, and Find They’re Equal

By/ Omar Mohamed Hussien Kotb


Abstract

Around 1600, more or less independently of the work in algebra, we see the first systematic use of ’the infinite’ in European mathematics; by the middle of the century, it had become common, and Pascal, a mathematical mystic, used it in a number of metaphorical statements (such as his famous ’wager’) and an early version of the calculus. Physical applications and the recognition that infinities in some sense underlie Archimedes’ work appear to be the primary impetus, although it may be necessary to be less cautious than he was in what one permits.
